Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) is a holistic approach that combines different strategies to reduce pest damage with minimal environmental impact. It includes physical barriers, biological controls like beneficial insects, and natural/organic remedies instead of relying solely on chemicals.

Physical Controls

Use of barriers, traps, and manual removal of pests to reduce infestations.

Biological Controls

Introducing beneficial insects such as ladybugs and parasitoids that prey on harmful pests.

Organic Remedies

Neem oil, garlic sprays, and other plant-based solutions to manage pests naturally.

Monitoring & Early Detection Tips

  • Regularly inspect crops for signs of pest damage.
  • Use sticky traps and light traps for early detection.
  • Record pest appearance patterns and seasons.
  • Maintain healthy soil and crop rotation to reduce pest buildup.
  • Encourage natural predators by planting flowering plants.

Effective Treatment Methods

Use low-toxicity sprays like neem oil, chili-garlic extracts, and soaps that are safe for crops and beneficial insects.

Introduce natural predators such as lacewings or praying mantis in affected areas to naturally control pest populations.
