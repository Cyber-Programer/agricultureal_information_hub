Crop Details

Explore varieties, cultivation, soil needs, harvesting, nutrition & uses

Wheat

Varieties: Durum, Bread Wheat

Soil: Loamy, well-drained

Cultivation: Rabi season, irrigated

Harvest: 110–120 days

Nutrition: Fiber, protein, B vitamins

Uses: Bread, pasta, flour

Mango

Varieties: Alphonso, Kesar, Dasheri

Soil: Alluvial, well-drained

Cultivation: Grafted saplings, summer watering

Harvest: 90–120 days after flowering

Nutrition: Vitamin C and A

Uses: Fresh, juices, pickles

Tomato

Varieties: Roma, Cherry, Heirloom

Soil: Sandy loam, well-drained

Cultivation: Requires staking & full sun

Harvest: 60–80 days

Nutrition: Lycopene, Vitamin C

Uses: Sauces, salads

Cotton

Varieties: BT Cotton, Hybrid

Soil: Black soil, well-drained

Cultivation: Kharif season, dry climate

Harvest: 150–180 days

Nutrition: Not consumable

Uses: Textile, oil, seed cake
